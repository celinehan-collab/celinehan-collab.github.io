This site is the individual portfolio for the Individual Website deliverable in EST 240. It houses three artifacts, the research essay Inhabiting the Scholar, the AI-generated personal mark documented on the Logo page, and the site itself, designed to satisfy the CARP principles of Contrast, Alignment, Repetition, and Proximity, and the CATN criteria of Content, Aesthetics, Technology, and Navigation that organize the course rubric.

❦

Why Ruby Granger

Among the candidate channels, Ruby Granger offers a particularly clear test case for visual rhetoric in 2026. She reviews study routines, morning rituals, and academic milestones using the same broad genre conventions other StudyTube creators rely upon, yet she sets herself apart through unusually disciplined mise-en-scène, a controlled Edwardian costume vocabulary, and a calm host persona whose self-presentation aligns precisely with the cultural memory of the fictional Hermione Granger. The channel provides a clean laboratory for reading the same set of frameworks the course foregrounds, film theory, Chandler’s semiotics, and Pennings’s anchoring tradition.

How the design serves the argument

The visual register of this site is not a neutral container for the argument. It is a deliberate echo of the Edwardian-romantic vocabulary the expertise paper attributes to Ruby Granger’s channel. The baroque oval monogram, the warm cream background, the soft peach accent, the burnished antique gold, and the single burgundy ruby on the crest of the mark have all been chosen so that the site inhabits the same aesthetic tradition it analyzes. The design, in this sense, is an extension of the argument rather than a frame around it.

Within that aesthetic register, the site implements the four CARP principles directly. Contrast sets serif display type (Cormorant Garamond) against a sans interface (Inter), warm brown text against cream background, and reserves burgundy for emphasis rather than spreading it across the page. Alignment uses a single left-aligned reading column for all body content, with center alignment reserved for hero sections and the royal seal in the footer, where centering functions as a signal of ceremony rather than reading flow. Repetition carries the same navigation, the same royal hero structure, the same footer royal seal, and the same type system through every page. Proximity groups essay metadata tightly under the title, clusters references at the foot of the essay, and places logo specifications immediately above the size variants they describe.

The four CATN criteria are met as follows. Content is the 9,100-word expertise paper with 29 references and in-text bracketed citations drawn from twenty-one authors, the logo prompts disclosed in full so that the AI tool remains transparent. Aesthetics follow the Edwardian-romantic register described above. Technology is hand-coded semantic HTML5 and CSS3 with no JavaScript framework dependency, served from GitHub Pages and responsive down to roughly 360 pixel viewports. Navigation uses a top bar replicated on every page with the active page marked, and a three-column footer that links to internal pages and to the two Pennings articles that define the course assignment.

About Jooyeon

Jooyeon Han is a student in the Department of Technology and Society at the State University of New York, Korea. Her coursework spans visual rhetoric, calculus, meteorology, science and technology studies, and introductory engineering, a breadth she has come to understand as deliberate rather than accidental. She is drawn to the places where the technical and the humanistic meet, the moments where two disciplines describe the same thing in different vocabularies and the difference becomes a way of seeing.

Colophon

The display face on this site is Cormorant Garamond, a contemporary revival of the sixteenth-century Garamond by Christian Thalmann. Body text is set in EB Garamond, a digitization of the original Garamond by Georg Duffner. Interface elements are set in Inter, a contemporary sans-serif by Rasmus Andersson designed for screen use. All fonts are open source and served through Google Fonts. The color palette draws on warm autumnal tones, cream, soft peach, antique gold, dark brown, chosen for visual compatibility with the autumn morning routines that organize much of the analyzed channel’s footage. The Latin motto Per signa, significatio means through signs, meaning, and the three-word tagline Aesthetic · Analysis · Anchor names the three operations the site performs upon its subject. The site contains no analytics scripts or tracking pixels.
