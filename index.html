The channel under analysis

973K Subscribers
146M Total Views
900+ Videos
2013 Channel Founded

Ruby Granger built her channel around a literary scholarly persona organized by the borrowed name of the fictional Hermione Granger. Her first viral video, How to Study Like Hermione Granger (2017), surpassed 150,000 views and established the visual codes that organize hundreds of subsequent uploads. The channel sits within the StudyTube niche traced by Lawless (2020) to the late 2010s, and its aesthetic discipline across nearly a decade makes it a productive object of visual rhetoric analysis.

Denotation fixes meaning, connotation communicates it. Anthony J. Pennings · EST 240 Visual Rhetoric and IT

The argument, in one paragraph

The expertise paper argues that Ruby Granger's channel succeeds because its three M's, namely the host, the mise-en-scène, and the montage, work in tight coordination to construct a single coherent identity, namely that of a contemporary literary scholar in the Edwardian-romantic register. The host functions as the anchor that Stuart Hall describes, the mise-en-scène functions as the affective stage that Bordwell and Thompson identify in classical film theory, and the montage functions according to the principles of meaning-construction first formulated by Lev Kuleshov and Sergei Eisenstein. The paper closes by suggesting that the most rigorous reading of any successful YouTube channel requires holding two registers in mind at once, namely the descriptive register that records what the channel does and the critical register that asks whose interests its codes serve.
